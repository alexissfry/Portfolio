Problem

Partiful is a venture-backed, social event planning platform that targets Gen Z and Millennial users. Compared to competitors like Eventbrite and Facebook, Partiful is highly customizable with trendy graphics and themes, enabling users to truly capture the vibe of their event and delight their guests. To scale after their series-A, the startup is hoping to increase user traction and engagement, and we are aiming to deliver customization features that do just that.

Scope

Since Partiful has an established platform and design system, our aim was not a complete redesign. Instead, our goal was to introduce new features on top of the existing interface and functionality that would enhance the user experience of event page customization. Further, I mainly took ownership over the title font customization and ‚ÄúPartiful your Guests‚Äù cover image features.

Research

As we are all users of Partiful‚Äôs product, we excitedly explored the existing event customization page, putting ourselves in the shoes of a Partiful employee to prioritize feasibility and a user to enable out-of-the-box thinking. We kicked off the ideation process by having a conversation about any thoughts each of us had to further customization. This multi-hour meeting led to a laundry list of ideas.

  • Incorporating music into the invite pages sent to guests
  • Title font customization
  • Animated or interactive cover images (e.g. by including guest faces in the image like Elf Yourself)
  • Custom page theme generators, either through AI or a color/gradient selector
  • Custom emojis/messages for RSVP-ing to events
  • Custom layout options for invite pages, possibly creatable via a drag-and-drop interface

Process

Sketching

We began to bring our ideas to life through roughly sketching features that would require meaningful adjustments to the existing interface: title font customization, animated or interactive cover images, music integration, and theme generation. Each member did their own sketches, which allowed us to explore different avenues of implementation.

When evaluating sketches, we decided to narrow the scope and ultimately forward went with title font customization, music integration, and theme generation through AI and color selection. We decided to cut animated or interactive cover images since these images are already customizable and, therefore, assumed this idea would be less of a priority from a business perspective.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

Wireframing allowed us to put these feature implementations and test their cohesion on the interface, which was especially important given the emphasis Partiful puts on WYSIWYG in their design philosophy.

We then presented our wireframed ideas to our clients and peers in a Loom. We were lucky to receive the following feedback :)

Takeaways

‚û°Ô∏è Remember to weigh technical feasibility

Lots of the feedback we got during our final demo to the Partiful team was questioning technical feasibility and also usability. In particular, we did not fully think through how the hue overlay would be built. Wearing different hats as a designer, especially before creating high-fidelity mockups, is incredibly important, because if an idea is not technically feasible it will not enter development.

‚û°Ô∏è Feedback is a gift

The most valuable part of the iterative design process was receiving really specific, thoughtful feedback from our points of contact. We saw how important good feedback is as it illuminated crucial blind spots for us to consider that enabled us to refine and ultimately design a better interface. Although we know that Partiful is more focused on building out the robustness of user networking on the platform right now, we hope that some of our ideas are implemented at some point. Regardless, I want to give Partiful a huge shoutout for giving us this wonderful opportunity to work with them on this project!
