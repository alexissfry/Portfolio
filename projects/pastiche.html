Problem

Although Pastiche Fine Desserts is a Federal Hill staple, their website is difficult to navigate and learn, has accessibility issues, is not responsive, and fails to embody the coziness and European essence of this beloved bakery.

Process

Ideation & Sketching

The 9-box speed sketch was an opportunity to put pen to paper on any interface designs that came to mind for Pastiche. I ended up gravitating towards a very minimalistic landing page that embodied the European-style of Pastiche and invited users to enter the website like you would the bakery itself.

With the final sketch, I decided to start by presenting not just their pastries but the store itself and its history. Then, I focused on making sure there were clear divisions between information. Sketching out the full interface gave me a thorough mental model of what I wanted to accomplish with the low-fidelity wireframes.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

These wireframes were essential in the process of refining the design to make sure I was addressing user pain points and planning out responsiveness from desktop to mobile interfaces.

Style Guide

High-fidelity Prototype

High contrast text

The interface uses high contrast colors, cream white and navy, for font and background. This change applies to the navbar dropdown, each section, and the footer.

Using icons for memorability

To declutter the landing page, I opted for a minimized navbar that could be maximized through clicking a hamburger icon. Further, the clickability through ‚ÄúFavorites‚Äù are denoted by arrow icons. Using icons allows the interface to efficiently convey functionality to the user through metaphorical affordance.

Ensuring responsiveness

During development, I focused on ensuring that each component is responsive to the smallest mobile device to the largest desktop. This technical functionality has important implications for accessibility and, ultimately, Pastiche‚Äôs brand credibility.

Takeaways

‚û°Ô∏è The importance responsiveness

Through learning by doing, I saw the simultaneous challenge and importance of interface responsiveness. Responsiveness demands creative thinking that balances conveying a component in the same way but with less space, which increases design and development time. In the specific use case of Pastiche, the ability to view the website on mobile devices is incredibly important for discoverability, since the chance that users are on their phones searching for pastry places is high. The lack of responsiveness of Pastiche‚Äôs website limits access to information for a subset of users and potentially could turn potential customers away, impacting Pastiche‚Äôs bottom line.

‚û°Ô∏è Getting feedback early

Since I was working on this project solo, I felt that deciding between ideas became difficult without input from another. Therefore, I took the initiative to finish my research and low-fidelity wireframes early and go to office hours. Through managing the timeline of my redesign well, I was able to get pivotal advice that helped improve the end product. I saw first-hand how getting feedback early on in the product development cycle and making changes then saves resources overall.
