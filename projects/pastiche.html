Pastiche Fine Desserts

A responsive redesign for Federal Hill's beloved dessert shop

Pastiche's current homepage, view the entire site here!

Overview

Although Pastiche truly sells impeccable pastries, their website is difficult to navigate and learn, has accessibility issues, is not responsive, and fails to embody the coziness and European essense of this Federal Hill staple.

Objective

The objective of this redesign is to make Pastiche's corner of the internet embody the experience of walking into their shop‚Äîthe smell of butter and sugar wafting from freshly baked pastries and the intricate painted murals of rolling green hills‚Äîto invite new and loyal customers alike inside. Secondly, this redesign will focus on redesigning outdated components to ensure that this 40-year old bakery is able stay relevant in our increasingly digital world.

Scope

This project exclusively focuses on redesigning the homepage of Pastiche's website, which is why some clickable links are just simulated.

Design Process

Early Stage Sketches

The starting, 9-box sketch was an opportunity to put pen to paper on any interface designs that came to mind for Pastiche! After reflecting on my initial, rough ideas, I gravitated towards a very minimalistic landing page that embodied the European-style of Pastiche and invited users to enter the website like you would the bakery itself.

With the final sketch, I decided to start by presenting photos of not just the desserts Pastiche sells but the store itself and its history. Then, I focused on making sure there were clear divisions between information. Sketching out the full interface gave me a thorough mental model of what I wanted to accomplish in the low-fidelity wireframes.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

The low-fidelity wireframes were essential in the process of refining the design to make sure I was addressing user pain points identified during research and planning out responsiveness across desktop and mobile interfaces. Then, I was able to move onto making all of my ideas come to life in the high-fidelity wireframes, accompanied by the style guide.

Style Guide

High-fidelity Prototypes

The high-fidelity prototypes fully implement the general layout of the low-fidelity wireframes, all the components and attributes in the style guide, and aims to embody the spirit of Pastiche, Providence's historic, European-style bakery shop. Certain components of the interface were designed intentionally to accomplish my objective:

  • Title fonts: Sticking with the original "Pastiche" logo font allows consistentcy throughout the interface and gives a established feel
  • Font color and size: The deep navy blue and cream create strong contrast for readibility and font size is scaled dynamically depending on the interface dimensions
  • About section image carousel and description: The description alongside images of Pastiche's storefront, bakers, and interior immediately give the users an understanding of the bakery and what it has to offer
  • Favorites section and order button: The floating nature of the cakes allow users to easily view customer favorites and the constrasting, centered order button gives an easy solution for users to satisfy their sweet tooth
  • Complete, scaled footer: The completeness and organization of the footer decreases user confusion and presents information that is easy to understand and interact with

Conclusion

I found this project to be quite challenging given that it was my first time using Bootstrap and media queries! At the same time, the process of redesigning an interface was really rewarding. More specifically, I really enjoyed the process of researching and designing a digital interface from scratch and developing it into a relatively functional and responsive final website. I found it especially satisfying because I am often annoyed how I can never provide direct feedback when interfaces frustrate me. I loved how I could take it into my own hands with this project, and I also learned really useful UX principles from sketching to coding the final product.
